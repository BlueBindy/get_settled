How do I apply for naturalisation?

Applications are made by paper form

Adult British citizens should use Form 8 (version 6.7 July 2022) which is found here. You will need to attach documents to evidence your residency in Ireland, based on the scorecard approach introduced in January 2022. You'll need documents worth 150 points for your required residency; the scorecard can be found here. Finally, you'll also need the application fee, a certified copy of your passport/s covering your residency your birth certificate and two witnessed photographs.

When can I apply?

Residents need 5 years residency and spouses of Irish citizens need 3 years

If you are married to an Irish citizen and living in Ireland, you'll need to prove 3 years residency. All other British citizens will need to prove they have lived in Ireland for 5 years. The current time to process a straightforward application submitted from 2022 onwards averages 19 months. So, between residency and application it is roughly a 5 to 7 year process. Please note, if you have Irish parents or grandparents you should use the Foreign Birth Registration process as you need to prove ancestry not residency.
