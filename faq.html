What is the 6 week rule I hear about?

Applicants face a limit on time spent out of Ireland

To qualify for citizenship by residency, you must prove that you have 'reckonable residence' in Ireland for at least 5 years (1825 or 1826 days if any leap years) out of the last 9 years. This includes 1 year (365 or 366 days if a leap year) of 'continuous residence' immediately before the date you apply. 'Continuous residence' doesn't mean you can't leave Ireland at all in your final year. It means you must have been physically in Ireland for at least 323 days of the last 365. In other words, you can not be away for more than 6 weeks (42 days) in your final year. This 6 week rule was established by the High Court.

Do I need to observe the 6 week rule after I've applied?

You can travel after application, but you need to notify the Department of Justice.

The Minister for Justice has confirmed the 6 week rule no longer applies after your application is submitted. However, if you are away from Ireland for longer than 6 weeks after application you need to notify the Department of Justice (email citizenshipinfo@justice.ie quoting your Application ID number) explaining the purpose of your trip. It is unclear why notification of absence is required, but it may relate to the time limits placed on candidate responses to Department correspondence.
