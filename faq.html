What is the 6 week rule I hear about?

There is a limit of time spent out of state

To qualify for citizenship by residency, you must prove that you have been resident in Ireland for at least 5 years (1825 or 1826 days if any leap years) out of the last 9 years. This includes 1 year (365 or 366 days if a leap year) of 'continuous residence' immediately before the date you apply. 'Continuous residence' doesn't mean you can't leave Ireland at all in your final year. To have your last year recognised as reckonable residence, 'continuous residence' you must have been physically in Ireland for at least 323 days; that is, not away for more than 6 weeks (42 days.) This 6 week rule was established in a High Court ruling.

Do I need to observe the 6 week rule after I've applied?

The 6 week rule applies post application

The Department of Justice are clear that if you are away from Ireland for longer than 6 weeks after you have applied you need to notify them ( use citizenshipinfo@justice.ie quoting your Application ID number if you have one) and explain why. However, the Minister for Justice has confirmed the 6 week rule no longer applies. The need to notify the Department may relate to the time limits given to candidates when responding to Department correspondence.

Good luck with your application!
